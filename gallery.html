CREATIVE LIFE CYCLE

Studio Transformation Journey

How raw earth changes into a glittering work of art through our five-stage artisanal process

Stage 01

Raw Piece (Clay Throwing)

Room Temp (68°F) Potter's Wheel 15-30 Mins

The journey begins with wet clay centered on a spinning pottery wheel. Using steady hands and water, we shape raw earth into cylinders, plates, and organic containers.

Artisanal Insight: Requires centering force and proper hand lubrication to keep clay wall thickness uniform.
01
02
Stage 02

Surface Painting

Bisque Fired (1700°F) Detail Brushes 1-3 Hours

After drying and a first "bisque" fire, the hardened, chalky white piece is ready. Customers use detail brushes and vibrant underglazes to hand-paint unique designs, floral patterns, or messages.

Artisanal Insight: Porous bisque clay absorbs pigment quickly, making steady brush strokes critical.
Stage 03

Liquid Glazing

Liquid Glaze suspension Dipping Tongs 5-10 Mins

The painted pottery is dipped into a vat of liquid glass suspension (glaze). The chalky liquid coats the pigments. When fired, this chalk melts into a waterproof, high-gloss food-safe glass seal.

Artisanal Insight: Proper dipping speed ensures an even thickness and prevents runoffs during final fire.
03
04
Stage 04

Extreme Kiln Firing

2200°F (Cone 5/6) Kiln Shelves 24-Hour Cycle

Pieces are loaded into our high-temperature kilns, firing up to 2200°F (1200°C). Over 24 hours, the intense heat triggers chemical transformations, fusing clay particles and glazing the surface.

Artisanal Insight: Firing vitrifies the silica, fusing paint pigments and glaze into a permanent glass matrix.
Stage 05

The Finished Masterpiece

Glossy & Safe Inspection Light Lifetime Heirloom

Once the kiln cools down, the finished pieces are unpacked. The raw, grey clay and powdery paint have transformed into a brilliant, glass-finished heirloom ready to take home and use daily.

Artisanal Insight: Food-safe, microwave-safe, dishwasher-safe, and ready to stand the test of time.

Ocean Whisper Technical Details
Clay Body Grolleg English Kaolin Porcelain (smooth white)
Technique Wheel-thrown, thin-walled construction
Decor Cobalt blue carbonate underglaze wash, custom hand-painted lines
Glaze High gloss, lead-free borosilicate clear coat
Firing Cone 10 oxidation gas kiln, peak 2350°F (1285°C)

Elena's Ocean Whisper Vase required 14 hours of air-drying before receiving its bisque fire, followed by fine brush decoration which took over 4 hours of painting time.

Artisan Painted Mugs Technical Details
Clay Body Buff Stoneware with iron spots (tactile sand texture)
Technique Wheel-thrown body, hand-pulled ergonomic handles
Decor Hand-painted slip-trailed cobalt blue patterns
Glaze Gloss clear interior, satin matte white exterior finish
Firing Cone 6 oxidation in electric kiln, peak 2232°F (1222°C)

Sarah's hand-painted mugs feature a comfortable thumb-rest handle and raw bottom ring showing the natural toasted clay speckles after firing.

Volcanic Ash Basin Technical Details
Clay Body Buff Stoneware with 15% medium grog (rough dark texture)
Technique Coil-built and paddle-shaped slab base
Decor Carved deep facets, slip-trailed iron oxides
Glaze Natural wood-ash overlay glaze with copper carbonate specks
Firing Cone 6 reduction atmosphere, peak 2232°F (1222°C)

The natural wood-ash melts inside the hot kiln atmosphere to run down the carved ridges, creating the signature bubbling volcanic lava textures.

Dynasty Blossom Vase Technical Details
Clay Body Grolleg English Kaolin Porcelain (smooth pure white)
Technique Wheel-thrown body, hand-sculpted elegant side handles
Decor Intricate hand-carved floral reliefs (sgraffito detailing)
Glaze Celadon green copper-crackle glaze suspension
Firing Cone 10 reduction gas kiln, peak 2350°F (1285°C)

Chen Wei's masterpiece requires over 40 hours of meticulous carving. The crackle patterns develop as the glass contracts faster than the porcelain body during cooling.
