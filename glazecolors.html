WHAT HAPPENS IN THE FIRE

Chemistry of Kiln Melting

When glazes are heated to 2200°F, mineral oxides decompose and fuse into colored glass. Here's the journey of firing.

01

Water Smoking (212°F - 392°F)

Physical water chemically bound inside the clay pores escapes as steam. Slow heating prevents cracks.

02

Quartz Inversion (1063°F)

Silica quartz crystals expand by about 1% in size, rearranging their crystal structure. The clay body becomes rigid.

03

Vitrification & Glaze Melt (1800°F - 2232°F)

Glaze powders melt into a liquid coat, adhering to the vitrified clay body. As it cools, it freezes into shiny glass.

TEMPERATURE STAGES

Firing Temperatures Compared

Different clays and glazes require specific heat levels to reach maturity. We fire to Cone 06 and Cone 6.

STAGE 1: LOW FIRE

Bisque Firing
(Cone 06 - 1828°F)

This initial bake hardens raw clay into porous ceramic, removing chemical water. It is durable enough to hold glaze without dissolving.

  • 12-hour heating ramp
  • 24-hour cooling window
  • Keeps pieces porous for glaze absorption
STAGE 2: HIGH FIRE

Glaze Firing
(Cone 6 - 2232°F)

This final fire vitrifies the clay body into solid stoneware and melts the glaze minerals into glossy glass. It renders the pieces completely waterproof.

  • 8-hour intense heating cycle
  • Completely vitrifies clay
  • Dinnerware and dishwasher safe
KNOW YOUR POTS

Glaze Care & Food Safety

Answers to common questions regarding lead content, food compatibility, and washing.

Yes! Every glaze used in the Clay & Kiln studio is 100% lead-free, non-toxic, and conforming to ASTM D-4236 safety standards.

Any glaze marked "Food Safe" on the jar is perfect for dinnerware, cups, and bowls once fired to Cone 6. Crackle glazes are decorative only.

Yes, stoneware fired in our studio can go in the top rack of the dishwasher. Handwashing is recommended for delicate handles.

Yes, but avoid extreme heat shocks (e.g. freezer to boiling oven) to prevent structural cracking. Avoid metallic luster paints in microwaves.
